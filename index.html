Working Paper

Global Warming, Ecosystems, and Inequality

with Rémi Turquier

In this paper we study how inequality interacts with the social cost of ecosystem losses in a warming world.

Abstract

Global warming, biodiversity loss and inequalities are interconnected, yet rarely studied jointly. We investigate how damages to the environment change the social cost of inequalities, and how inequalities influence the social value of ecosystem services. We show that the answer depends on two parameters: inequality aversion, and substitutability between market goods and environmental goods. When both parameters are high, inequality increases the marginal value of the environment. This is not always the case when both parameters are low. We quantify this interaction by building a climate-economy model that includes ecosystem services and features both national and international inequalities. In our main calibration, the presence of national inequalities doubles the marginal social cost of damages to the environment. Overall, our findings show that inequalities and ecosystem services interact in large and non-obvious ways. Ignoring this interaction can lead to substantial errors in the evaluation of environmental and redistributive policies.

Work in Progress

Unintended consequences of nature-based climate mitigation: Exotic natural capital and water scarcity

with Bruno Pimenta

In this paper we examine whether industrial tree plantations generate unintended trade-offs between economic output and freshwater provision.

Abstract

Increasing the tree-covered area is often promoted as an effective policy to meet carbon mitigation goals. However, the expansion of exotic monoculture plantations can disrupt local ecosystem services. This paper studies the impact of these plantations on freshwater provision using watershed-level data and panel variation in land-cover change in Chile. Our initial results show that tree plantation expansion causally reduces surface freshwater availability, suggesting a trade-off between carbon sequestration and local water resources.
